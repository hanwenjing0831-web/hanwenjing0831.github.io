法庭意见书要素（英文）

Basic Information of the Case

  • Case Name: LOCHNER V. NEW YORK
  • Citation: 198 U.S. 45 (1905)
  • Court: Supreme Court of the United States
  • Argued Date: February 23, 24, 1905
  • Decided Date: April 17, 1905
  • Error to: The County Court of Oneida County, State of New York

Parties Involved

  • Plaintiff in Error: Lochner (owner of a bakery in New York, convicted of violating New York’s labor law by allowing an employee to work more than 60 hours a week)
  • Defendant in Error: State of New York (represented by Julius M. Mayer, Attorney General of New York)

Facts of the Case

  1. In 1897, New York enacted a labor law (Article 8, Section 110 of the Laws of 1897), which prohibited employers in bakeries from "requiring or permitting" employees to work more than 60 hours per week or 10 hours per day, with no provisions for special emergencies.
  2. Lochner, the owner of a bakery, was indicted for violating this law by allowing an employee to work more than 60 hours in one week. He argued that the law violated the 14th Amendment of the U.S. Constitution, which protects the right to liberty of contract between employers and employees.
  3. The lower courts (Supreme Court and Court of Appeals of New York) upheld the validity of the labor law, convicting Lochner. Lochner then appealed to the Supreme Court of the United States.

Legal Issues

  1. Whether the New York labor law, which limits the working hours of bakery employees, constitutes an unconstitutional interference with the "liberty of contract" protected by the Due Process Clause of the 14th Amendment.
  2. Whether the state’s exercise of police power (to protect public health, safety, morals, and general welfare) extends to regulating the working hours of bakery employees, or whether such regulation exceeds the scope of valid police power.

Majority Opinion (Delivered by MR. JUSTICE PECKHAM)

1. Core Holding: The New York labor law is unconstitutional because it arbitrarily interferes with the liberty of contract between employers and employees, which is protected by the 14th Amendment’s Due Process Clause.

2. Key Reasoning:

  • The general right to make contracts related to one’s business is part of the individual liberty protected by the 14th Amendment (citing Allgeyer v. Louisiana, 165 U.S. 578). The right to purchase or sell labor is within this protected liberty, unless there are circumstances justifying state interference.
  • The state’s police power is limited to regulating matters related to public health, safety, morals, and general welfare. However, baking is not an "unhealthy occupation" to a degree that justifies the state’s interference with the right of contract. The majority argued that:
    • There is no reasonable basis to claim that working more than 60 hours a week in a bakery endangers employees’ health. Unlike mining or smelting (which the Court had previously allowed to be regulated in Holden v. Hardy, 169 U.S. 366), baking does not involve inherent health risks that require hour limits.
    • The law does not address "cleanliness" or "sanitation" of bakeries (which could be valid police power regulations), but only limits working hours. Such a limitation has no direct relation to public health.
  • If the state could regulate the working hours of bakery employees under the guise of police power, there would be no limit to state interference with other occupations (e.g., printers, clerks, lawyers), which would undermine the 14th Amendment’s protection of liberty.

Dissenting Opinions

1. MR. JUSTICE HARLAN (with MR. JUSTICE WHITE and MR. JUSTICE DAY concurring):

  • The state’s police power includes the authority to regulate working hours to protect employees’ health, especially when the occupation involves potential health risks (e.g., long hours in a bakery’s hot, flour-dusty environment).
  • The legislature, not the courts, is the proper body to determine whether an occupation is unhealthy and whether regulation is necessary. The New York legislature’s judgment that bakery work requires hour limits is a valid exercise of power of its policy-making authority, and the courts should not substitute their judgment for the legislature’s.
  • Citing expert opinions (e.g., Professor Hirt’s Diseases of the Workers), the dissent argued that bakery work is laborious, often performed in overheated, dusty conditions, and long hours can lead to respiratory diseases, rheumatism, and shortened lifespans—justifying the state’s regulation.

2. MR. JUSTICE HOLMES:

  • The majority’s decision is based on an economic theory (laissez-faire) that is not embodied in the Constitution. The 14th Amendment does not "enact Mr. Herbert Spencer’s Social Statics" (a reference to Spencer’s laissez-faire philosophy).
  • The state has the right to enact laws that reflect the dominant public opinion of the time, even if judges disagree with the economic theory behind them. Sunday laws, usury laws, and vaccination laws (citing Jacobson v. Massachusetts, 197 U.S. 11) all interfere with liberty of contract but are valid exercises of state power.
  • A constitution is "made for people of fundamentally differing views," and the court should not strike down a state statute merely because it conflicts with the judges’ economic beliefs.

Judgment

The Supreme Court reversed the judgment of the Court of Appeals of New York and the County Court of Oneida County, holding that the New York labor law violated the 14th Amendment. Lochner’s conviction was overturned.

双语知识库

（一）术语（20个，含英文、中文释义及案例语境）

1. Liberty of Contract

合同自由（指雇主与雇员之间自主约定劳动条件的权利，是14修正案所保护的“个人自由”核心内容。案例中多数意见认为纽约州劳动法限制面包店员工工时，侵犯了这一权利）

*Context*: "The general right to make a contract in relation to his business is part of the liberty of the individual protected by the Fourteenth Amendment of the Federal Constitution."

2. Police Power

警察权（州政府为保护公共健康、安全、道德和公共福利而行使的固有权力，案例中纽约州主张其劳动法是警察权的合法行使）

*Context*: "Those powers, broadly stated and without, at present, any attempt at a more specific limitation, relate to the safety, health, morals and general welfare of the public."

3. Due Process Clause

正当法律程序条款（美国宪法第14修正案中的条款，禁止各州未经正当法律程序剥夺公民的生命、自由或财产，案例中原告以该条款为依据挑战州法）

*Context*: "Under that provision no State can deprive any person of life, liberty or property without due process of law."

4. Labor Law

劳动法（规范雇主与雇员之间劳动关系的法律，案例中争议的纽约州法律即属于劳动法范畴，限制了面包店的工时）

*Context*: "The indictment charged that the plaintiff in error violated the one hundred and tenth section of article 8, chapter 415, of the Laws of 1897, known as the labor law of the State of New York."

5. Indictment

起诉书（检察机关对犯罪嫌疑人提起公诉的法律文书，案例中原告因违反劳动法被提起公诉，相关指控载于起诉书中）

*Context*: "The indictment, it will be seen, charges that the plaintiff in error violated the one hundred and tenth section of article 8, chapter 415, of the Laws of 1897..."

6. Plaintiff in Error

上诉人（在上诉案件中提起上诉的一方，案例中Lochner作为原审被告，对判决不服提起上诉，故为上诉人）

*Context*: "Mr. Frank Harvey Field and Mr. Henry Weissmann for plaintiff in error."

7. Defendant in Error

被上诉人（在上诉案件中回应上诉的一方，案例中纽约州作为原审原告，回应Lochner的上诉，故为被上诉人）

*Context*: "Mr. Julius M. Mayer, Attorney General of the State of New York, for defendant in error."

8. Majority Opinion

多数意见（最高法院中多数法官出具的意见，具有法律效力，案例中Peckham大法官撰写的多数意见裁定纽约州劳动法违宪）

*Context*: "MR. JUSTICE PECKHAM, after making the foregoing statement of the facts, delivered the opinion of the court."

9. Dissenting Opinion

异议意见（最高法院中少数法官对多数意见的反对意见，无法律效力但具有参考价值，案例中Harlan大法官和Holmes大法官分别出具了异议意见）

*Context*: "MR. JUSTICE HARLAN, with whom MR. JUSTICE WHITE and MR. JUSTICE DAY concurred, dissenting."

10. Supreme Court of the United States

美国最高法院（美国联邦司法体系的最高审级，有权审查州法是否符合联邦宪法，案例即由该法院审理并作出最终判决）

*Context*: "LOCHNER V. NEW YORK 198 U.S. 45 (1905) Supreme Court of United States"

11. Court of Appeals

上诉法院（州司法体系中的上诉审法院，案例中原审判决由纽约州上诉法院作出，后被联邦最高法院推翻）

*Context*: "In the Supreme Court two of the five judges composing the Appellate Division dissented from the judgment affirming the validity of the act. In the Court of Appeals three of the seven judges also dissented."

12. County Court

郡法院（州司法体系中的基层法院，案例中Lochner最初在纽约州奥奈达郡法院被定罪）

*Context*: "ERROR TO THE COUNTY COURT OF ONEIDA COUNTY, STATE OF NEW YORK."

13. Unconstitutional

违宪的（指法律或政府行为违反宪法规定，案例中联邦最高法院裁定纽约州劳动法违宪）

*Context*: "We think that the limit of the police power has been reached and passed in this case... the act is not, within any fair meaning of the term, a health law, but is an illegal interference with the rights of individuals..."

14. Valid Exercise of Power

权力的合法行使（指政府行为符合宪法和法律规定，案例中异议意见认为纽约州劳动法是警察权的合法行使）

*Context*: "The act was held to be a valid exercise of the police powers of the State." (referring to Holden v. Hardy)

15. Public Health

公共健康（州警察权的核心保护对象之一，案例中纽约州主张工时限制是为了保护面包店员工的公共健康）

*Context*: "The law must be upheld, if at all, as a law pertaining to the health of the individual engaged in the occupation of a baker."

16. Occupation

职业（指个人从事的工作类型，案例中争议焦点是“面包师”这一职业是否需要通过州法限制工时）

*Context*: "the occupation of a baker, in and of itself, is not an unhealthy one to that degree which would authorize the legislature to interfere with the right to labor."

17. Statute

制定法（由立法机关制定的法律，案例中争议的纽约州劳动法即属于制定法）

*Context*: "Statutes of the nature of that under review, limiting the hours in which grown and intelligent men may labor to earn their living, are mere meddlesome interferences with the rights of the individual."

18. Appellate Jurisdiction

上诉管辖权（法院审查下级法院判决的权力，美国联邦最高法院对本案行使的即上诉管辖权）

*Context*: "We granted certiorari to review this important decision involving rights protected by the First and Fourteenth Amendments." (similar logic applies to appellate jurisdiction in this case)

19. Certiorari

调卷令（最高法院签发的命令，要求下级法院提交案件记录以供审查，案例中最高法院通过签发调卷令受理了本案）

*Context*: "ERROR TO THE COUNTY COURT OF ONEIDA COUNTY, STATE OF NEW YORK." (implied in the court’s acceptance of the appeal via certiorari)

20. Judgment Reversed

判决撤销（上级法院推翻下级法院的判决，案例中联邦最高法院撤销了纽约州法院对Lochner的定罪判决）

*Context*: "The judgment of the Court of Appeals of New York as well as that of the Supreme Court and of the County Court of Oneida County must be reversed and the case remanded to the County Court for further proceedings not inconsistent with this opinion."

（二）短语（10个，含英文、中文释义及案例语境）

1. Violate the labor law

违反劳动法（指行为人未遵守劳动法的规定，案例中原告因允许员工每周工作超过60小时而被指控违反纽约州劳动法）

*Context*: "The indictment charged that the plaintiff in error violated the one hundred and tenth section of article 8, chapter 415, of the Laws of 1897, known as the labor law of the State of New York."

2. Interfere with the liberty of contract

干涉合同自由（指政府或第三方对雇主与雇员之间自主约定劳动条件的权利进行限制，案例中多数意见认为纽约州劳动法构成了对合同自由的非法干涉）

*Context*: "The statute necessarily interferes with the right of contract between the employer and employees, concerning the number of hours in which the latter may labor in the bakery of the employer."

3. Exercise of police power

行使警察权（州政府为保护公共利益而采取的监管行为，案例中纽约州主张其工时限制是行使警察权的体现）

*Context*: "The State, therefore, has power to prevent the individual from making certain kinds of contracts, and in regard to them the Federal Constitution offers no protection if the contract be one which the State, in the legitimate exercise of its police power, has the right to prohibit."

4. Unhealthy occupation

有害健康的职业（指对从业者身体健康存在固有风险的职业，案例中多数意见认为面包师不属于此类职业）

*Context*: "We think that there can be no fair doubt that the trade of a baker, in and of itself, is not an unhealthy one to that degree which would authorize the legislature to interfere with the right to labor."

5. Due process of law

正当法律程序（宪法规定的公民权利，要求政府在剥夺公民权益时遵循合法程序，案例中原告主张纽约州劳动法未经正当程序剥夺了其合同自由）

*Context*: "Under that provision no State can deprive any person of life, liberty or property without due process of law."

6. Majority and dissenting opinions

多数意见与异议意见（最高法院法官对案件的不同观点，多数意见具有法律效力，异议意见反映不同立场，案例中双方围绕工时限制的合法性展开激烈争论）

*Context*: "In the Supreme Court two of the five judges composing the Appellate Division dissented from the judgment affirming the validity of the act. In the Court of Appeals three of the seven judges also dissented from the judgment upholding the statute."

7. Remand the case

发回案件（上级法院将案件退回下级法院，要求其根据上级法院的意见重新审理，案例中最高法院撤销原判后将案件发回郡法院）

*Context*: "The judgment of the Court of Appeals of New York as well as that of the Supreme Court and of the County Court of Oneida County must be reversed and the case remanded to the County Court for further proceedings not inconsistent with this opinion."

8. Public welfare

公共福利（政府行为的核心目标之一，州警察权的行使需以促进公共福利为目的，案例中纽约州主张工时限制有利于公共福利）

*Context*: "Those powers, broadly stated and without, at present, any attempt at a more specific limitation, relate to the safety, health, morals and general welfare of the public."

9. Constitutional right

宪法权利（由宪法赋予公民的基本权利，案例中原告主张合同自由是受14修正案保护的宪法权利）

*Context*: "The general right to make a contract in relation to his business is part of the liberty of the individual protected by the Fourteenth Amendment of the Federal Constitution."

10. Overturn the conviction

撤销定罪（上级法院推翻下级法院对被告人的有罪判决，案例中最高法院撤销了对Lochner的定罪）

*Context*: "The judgment of the Court of Appeals of New York as well as that of the Supreme Court and of the County Court of Oneida County must be reversed..." (reversal of the conviction is implied)
